🤔 Why Agnibha Should Come

🧠

He'll Learn Things School Can't Teach

Textbooks are great, but mountains teach something else entirely — patience, teamwork, problem-solving, and the ability to keep going even when things get tough. Honestly? These are the skills that stick for life. This isn't just a vacation, it's a crash course in growing up (the fun way).

👥

This Friend Group Won't Always Be Together

We know this sounds dramatic, but it's true — Class 12 is right around the corner, and after that it's colleges in different cities, new lives, new schedules. This trip is genuinely one of the last chances for all of us to just be together without the weight of boards hanging over us. We don't want to look back and wish we had gone.

💪

He's More Than Ready

Auntie ji, Agnibha hasn't needed his inhaler in over 2 years — that's not luck, that's growth. The air in Mukteshwar is genuinely clean and fresh, far better than what we breathe in Delhi every day. If anything, the mountains will do his lungs a favour, not a disservice. We promise.

📋 We Figured You'd Ask...

What about his health? (Asthma, etc.) 😤

We completely understand why you'd be concerned — and we want to put your mind at ease. Agnibha has been inhaler-free for over 2 years now, which is genuinely impressive. Mukteshwar is known for its exceptionally clean mountain air. We'll carry necessary medicines, have emergency contacts on hand, and there will be adult supervision throughout. You have our word he'll be looked after.

What about his studies? 📚

Four days, Auntie ji. That's all this is. Class 11 allows for that. In fact, a short mental break often leads to sharper focus when students return — it's genuinely backed by science. He'll come back refreshed, recharged, and honestly probably more motivated than before.

Is it actually safe? 🛡️

Yes, absolutely. This is an organized group trip — not a solo adventure. Experienced guides, proper accommodation, planned routes, and adult chaperones at every step. We're not "roughing it in the jungle." Think of it more like a structured, fun expedition.

Why this trip specifically? Why now? 🤨

Because Class 11 is the last real breathing room before Class 12 boards take over everything. Once that starts, this kind of trip won't be possible for years. This is the window — and we really don't want Agnibha to miss it. (Or us to miss him on it, honestly.)
